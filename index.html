Featured Research Projects

Cutting-edge applications of machine learning in biological sciences

Morphological Analysis of Retinal Pigment Epithelium

Developed an automated, config-driven 7-step end-to-end machine learning pipeline for classifying Retinal Pigment Epithelial (RPE) cells during aging. Extracted 133 morphological and texture-based features (LBP, GLCM, Gabor) from 326 RPE cell images, achieving 94%+ cross-validation F1-score using a stacking ensemble of XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Cancer Invasion

Engineered Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to simulate collective cancer invasion dynamics. Developed a framework utilizing Tensorized Fourier Neural Operators (TFNO) and PyTorch's autograd engine to encode physical laws directly into the model's loss function, providing data-efficient solutions for complex PDEs.
